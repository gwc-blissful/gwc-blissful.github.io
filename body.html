Stretching: An Illustrated Guide

Why Stretch?

Stretching exercises encourage lengthening of your muscles and their associated tendons. They counteract the shortening and tightening of muscles that can occur immediately after vigorous exercise, and as a product of ageing and inactivity. A sedentary lifestyle that involves long periods of sitting or driving can cause your muscles to shorten and tighten. This reduces your range of motion and can lead to stiffness and pain.

A Simple Static Stretching Routine

  • stretch only after warming up, or after exercise, when muscles are warn.
  • repeat each stretch 2 to 3 times, working both sides of the body equally.
  • hold each stretch for 10 to 30 seconds.
  • breathe freely while stretching.
  • do not bounce.

    • 1. Stretch Your Back

    Bend backward slightly while standing to stretch your back. Put your hands on your lower back. Tilt yourself back at the waist, lengthening the muscles. Make sure to keep the knees straight while doing this stretch.

    2. Stretch While Lying On Stomach

    Get down on your stomach with your arms to your sides and turn your head to one side. After a few minutes, move your arms so your elbows are still at your side but you're balanced on your forearms. Lift up the top half of your body like a seal, resting on your elbows and forearms.

    3. Standing Side Stretch

    Place your feet together on the floor. Bring your hands together and extend them upwards straight about your head, breathing in as you do. Tilt your body to the right side at your waist and hold this position. Do the same on the other side.
